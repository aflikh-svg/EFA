📌 Ancol Sentiong Pump Station – Design Change Adaptation

Economic & Technical Summary Report | Flood Mitigation & Infrastructure Resilience

🔹 Project ID: EFA Tool & Technical Specs Analysis | Year 2020-2022
01 Background & Urgency

Jakarta is located on the Ciliwung River delta, with ~40% of the area below sea level. Land subsidence in North and West Jakarta has made gravity-based drainage increasingly ineffective, causing recurrent severe floods (2002, 2007, 2013, 2014) with inundation up to 5 meters. The existing Ancol Pump Station cannot fully drain water from the Ciliwung, Gunung Sahari, and Kampung Bandan catchments when the Marina Sluice Gate is closed. The Sentiong Pump Station design change was introduced to address increasing flood risks, land subsidence, and tidal overflow while protecting 2,573 Ha of service area.

📊 Key facts: Service area: 2,573 Ha (25.73 km²) | Economic life: 30 years | Construction: 2021-2023
02 Scope of Work

🏗️ Civil & Structural Works

  • Pump house construction (Ancol Sentiong)
  • Normalization of Ancol River
  • Discharge pipelines extension
  • Cofferdam / sheet pile (temporary & permanent)
  • Secant pile & strauss pile foundations
  • Box culvert, retaining walls, and ground anchors

⚙️ Mechanical & Electrical

  • 5 axial submersible pumps (10 m³/s each, total 50 m³/s)
  • Medium & low voltage switchgears (3.3 kV / 6.6 kV)
  • 3 Diesel Engine Generators (2 x 2,500 kVA + 200 kVA)
  • Step-down transformers (200 kVA)
  • Overhead traveling cranes (35 ton & 15 ton)
  • Sluice gates, stop logs, and control systems (SCADA)

🔧 Additional: Land acquisition, river sediment dredging, environmental & safety management (SMKK), structural monitoring of adjacent toll road.

03 Objectives
  • Reduce annual flood damage in Utan Kayu, Sentiong, and Pademangan areas.
  • Maintain upstream water levels at HWL +0.64 m / NWL +0.14 m / LWL -2.36 m.
  • Prevent tidal backflow and improve drainage capacity from 0 m³/s to 50 m³/s.
  • Achieve economic viability with ENPV > 0, EIRR > 12%, BCR > 1.
  • Provide robust infrastructure resilient to climate change and land subsidence.
04 Methodology

📈 Economic & Financial Analysis (EFA)

  • Shadow exchange rate factor (SERF) derived from trade data.
  • Conversion factors for tradable/non-tradable goods & labor.
  • Financial – Economic cost conversion (tax adjustment, SWF).
  • Expected annual damage (building, agriculture, industry, toll road).
  • Discount rate: 12% – ENPV, EIRR, BCR calculated.

🌊 Technical & Hydrological Assessment

  • Flood depth-damage functions (0 – 6 m).
  • Sediment transport & bathymetric surveys.
  • Soil investigation (sondir, core drilling, SPT).
  • Climate change scenario (2050) vs base year 2020.
  • Monte Carlo / sensitivity analysis on costs & benefits.
05 Outputs
  • Physical outputs: 1 new pump station, 5 units axial pumps (10 m³/s each), 2.4 km discharge pipeline, 2 sluice gates, fully automated control room.
  • Economic outputs: ENPV = Rp 55.29 billion (positive), EIRR = 15.1% (>12% discount factor), BCR = 1.31 (>1).
  • Construction physical progress: 30% (Y1) – 40% (Y2) – 30% (Y3).
  • Routine O&M cost: 2.5% of investment; periodic O&M (every 5 years): 7.5%.
  • Complete as-built drawings, operation manuals, and operator training.
06 Outcomes

✅ Immediate outcomes

  • Flood depth reduced to <0.5m in previously inundated zones (Q25 return period).
  • Expected annual damage (buildings + agriculture) reduced from ~Rp 51.15B to near zero for design events.
  • Avoided toll road closure losses (annual ~Rp 10.9B).
  • Protection for ~57,000+ people in Ancol, Pademangan Barat, Pademangan Timur.

📊 Economic outcomes

  • Positive net cash flow throughout operation phase.
  • Indirect benefits: residential loss reduction (50%), reduced industrial loss (75%), lower economic disruption (35%).
  • Beneficial spillover for adjacent infrastructure (toll road, port activities).
07 Impact
  • Social: Improved public safety, reduced displacement during monsoon, lower disease risk from stagnant water.
  • Economic: Avoided losses of industrial/commercial output – supports Jakarta’s economic hub resilience (estimated annual benefit > Rp 50 billion).
  • Environmental: Controlled sediment flushing, reduced uncontrolled flooding, improved water quality management.
  • Institutional: Strengthens flood forecasting and early warning capacity (SCADA integration).
  • Climate adaptation: Design accounts for sea-level rise and increased rainfall intensity (scenario 2050).
08 Sustainability
  • Financial sustainability: O&M budget allocation from DGWR & BBWSCC (average ~1.5% of total budget) – routine and periodic maintenance plan embedded.
  • Technical sustainability: Operator training, spare parts supply, regular sediment transport monitoring every 5 years.
  • Institutional sustainability: Transfer of as-built documents, SCADA system handover, collaboration with local water resources agency.
  • Economic sustainability: Positive ENPV and high BCR ensure long-term net benefits. Sensitivity analysis shows project remains feasible under +10% cost overrun or delay.
09 Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

🔍 Key lessons

  • Integrating economic analysis early (EFA tool) helps quantify flood damage avoided and justifies high upfront investment.
  • Land subsidence requires combination of polder system + pumps, not only river normalization.
  • Climate change scenarios (2050) significantly increase expected annual damage without project – proactive design is cost‑effective.
  • Sensitivity analysis: investment cost overrun has highest impact on ENPV; still viable up to +20% overrun.

📌 Policy recommendations

  • Mandate economic EFA for all major flood control infrastructure in subsidence zones.
  • Establish dedicated O&M endowment fund (at least 2.5% of asset value annually).
  • Adopt updated flood depth-damage curves for Jakarta using real BNPB data.
  • Incorporate climate change multipliers (SLR, extreme rainfall) into design standards.
  • Strengthen coordination between PUPR, local government, and PLN for reliable power supply to pump stations.
10 Brief Policy Summary
📋 Policy brief: The Ancol Sentiong Pump Station design change is economically viable (EIRR 15.1%, BCR 1.31) and technically essential. It provides Rp 55.3 billion net present value over 30 years. Recommended actions: fast-track implementation, secure annual O&M budget (≈Rp 10–12 billion/year), and replicate this polder+ pump model in other subsiding coastal areas (e.g., Muara Baru, Kapuk). Integrate real‑time monitoring and enforce land use controls in floodplains to sustain benefits.
