Background & Urgency

Indonesia holds 18% of world’s coral reefs but ~70% are threatened by destructive fishing, pollution, and climate pressures. High coastal poverty and limited MPA financing required an integrated solution.

Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations
Key lessons
  • Existence value (EV) drives economic returns – rare species matter for EIRR.
  • PES and blue carbon are viable financing instruments for MPAs.
  • Long-term O&M budget sensitivity > investment cost sensitivity.
  • Gender inclusion amplifies project effectiveness and ownership.
Policy brief takeaways
  • Scale PES nationwide & integrate carbon credits.
  • Mandate non-use value (EV ≥ 18%) in economic appraisal of MPAs.
  • Allocate dedicated O&M budgets + technology (IoT/drones).
  • Replicate community-based enterprises with digital market access.
Policy impact: COREMAP-CTI provides a replicable model for achieving SDG 14, low-carbon development, and sustainable blue economy.

Blue Economy & Replication Potential

COREMAP-CTI demonstrates that integrated MPA management + PES + women-led enterprises can deliver sustained environmental and economic returns. The framework is ready for national scaling under Indonesia’s 32.5M ha MPA roadmap and Coral Triangle Initiative.

Potential impact if replicated: Mobilize USD 2B+ in blue carbon credits & ecotourism revenues by 2030.
