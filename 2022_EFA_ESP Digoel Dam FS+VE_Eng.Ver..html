📊 Value Engineering Summary Report

Digoel Multipurpose Dam – Boven Digoel District, Papua Province

Final Recommendation – Case 2-1 (EL +59m)

🔹 1. Background & Urgency

Papua Island faces significant infrastructure gaps despite its abundant natural resources. While the Trans-Papua road, airports, and basic facilities have expanded, no multipurpose dam exists in Papua to date. The region suffers from limited electricity (only a small fraction enjoys 24h power), unreliable irrigation, and vulnerability to floods. The Digoel Dam is planned on the Muyu River (Ninati District, Boven Digoel) near the Indonesia–PNG border — a strategic location to boost national resilience, food/water/energy security, and equitable development.

⚡ Critical needs: irrigation for 6,090 Ha, raw water supply (32.25 L/s), hydropower (13.96 MW planned), and flood mitigation. Without the dam, agricultural productivity and energy access will remain severely constrained.

📌 2. Scope

The Value Engineering (VE) study covered the entire design and cost structure of the Digoel earthfill dam, including alternative dam types (rockfill, concrete, CFRD), five dam crest elevations (+52m to +67m), economic & financial analysis (ENPV, EIRR, BCR), and socio-environmental impacts. The analysis focused on optimizing the balance between cost, performance, and long-term benefits.

🎯 3. Objectives

  • Identify the most cost-effective dam alternative without compromising safety or functionality.
  • Maximize economic returns (EIRR ≥9% as per ADB guidelines) and benefit-cost ratio.
  • Ensure technical feasibility, material availability, and minimal social disruption.
  • Support national Nawacita mission for Papua prosperity through integrated water, food, and energy infrastructure.

⚙️ 4. Methodology

Five-phase Value Engineering approach (Information, Creative, Analysis, Development, Recommendation) combined with quantitative descriptive methods. The team comprised 10 experts (dam engineering, hydrology, geotechnics, economics, environment). Economic analysis used ENPV, EIRR, and EBCR, plus a “matrix evaluation” (weighted multi-criteria) to compare alternatives based on cost, benefits (irrigation, hydropower, water conservation), and social impacts (land acquisition, flood risk).

📦 5. Output

Selected Ninati Dam Case 2-1 with characteristics:

  • Dam Height: +31m, Crest Elevation: +59m
  • Hydropower potential: 6.2 MW (vs. 4 MW in Case-1)
  • Raw water volume: 40.91 million m³
  • Total investment after tax: Rp 927.27 billion (slightly higher than cheapest case, but far better economic returns)
✅ EIRR = 18.46% (exceeds 12% feasibility threshold)
✅ ENPV = Rp 766.19 × 10⁶
✅ EBCR = 1.97 (every Rp1 yields Rp1.97 benefit)

🏆 6. Outcome

Despite Case-1 (EL+52m) having the lowest construction cost, the VE team recommended Case 2-1 (EL+59m) because of superior socio-economic performance. Matrix evaluation total score: Case 2-1 = 423 points vs. Case-1 = 373 points. The added height yields more reliable dry-season water, higher clean energy output, and better flood control — crucial for Papua’s growing needs. The rockfill dam type was confirmed as most suitable based on local material availability and foundation conditions.

🌍 7. Impact

💧 Water & Food
Irrigation for 6,090 Ha → increased food security, reduced imports. Reliable raw water for 104,000+ people.
⚡ Energy
6.2 MW renewable hydro → 24h electricity for thousands of households, supports local industries.
🌊 Resilience
Flood reduction in downstream areas; groundwater conservation through reservoir seepage.
📈 Economic
EIRR 18.46% → high development impact; creates jobs, stimulates local economy near border region.

♻️ 8. Sustainability

The rockfill dam design uses local river aggregates, reducing carbon footprint from long-distance hauling. Reservoir management plan includes sediment control and environmental flow to maintain river ecology. Operation & maintenance (O&M) procedures are designed for long-term reliability. The hydropower component provides clean energy, displacing diesel generators. Community engagement and land acquisition are minimal (only small areas for access roads).

📚 9. Lesson Learning & Policy Recommendations

  • Beyond least-cost: Initial lowest capital cost may not deliver optimal national benefits. Policy should require multi-criteria analysis (including EIRR, social, resilience) for infrastructure in remote regions.
  • Value Engineering is critical: Mandate VE for all major dams — it revealed a 6.2 MW vs 4 MW option that changed the project’s viability.
  • Border development: Infrastructure near international borders (PNG) needs higher design standards to support sovereignty and equitable growth.
  • Local materials & workforce: Rockfill dam reduces dependency on imported concrete; policies should incentivize local sourcing.
  • Integrated planning: Water-energy-food nexus approach is essential: the same dam serves multiple SDGs, improving fiscal efficiency.

📋 10. Brief Policy (Executive Summary for Decision Makers)

🔹 Policy Brief – Digoel Dam (Case 2-1, EL+59m)

▶ Adopt Ninati Dam Case 2-1 (Height +31m, crest EL+59m) as the official design.
▶ Allocate budget: Rp 927.27 billion (including tax) — offers EIRR 18.46%, BCR 1.97, and 6.2 MW renewable power.
▶ Accelerate land acquisition for the modest affected parcels; secure community support.
▶ Require VE for all future dams in eastern Indonesia to maximize socio-economic returns.
▶ Coordinate with Ministry of Energy & Agriculture to ensure irrigation and hydropftower integration by 2027.
▶ Establish long-term O&M fund from hydropower revenues to sustain dam operations.
