🌾 Pelosika & Rongkong Irrigation Systems

Master Plan & Feasibility Study Summary | Project No. 3455 - INO | February 2021

Directorate of Irrigation and Lowland, DGWR, MPWH - ADB Technical Assistance

1. Background & Urgency

Indonesia faces persistent rural poverty—1.8 times higher than in urban areas—and food insecurity despite positive agricultural trends. Only 55% of irrigation systems operate at full capacity, limiting crop intensity and yields. Climate change further exacerbates water uncertainty. The Pelosika (Southeast Sulawesi) and Rongkong (South Sulawesi) irrigation projects aim to address these challenges by improving water infrastructure, expanding irrigated area, and increasing agricultural productivity to strengthen national food security and rural livelihoods.

📊 Key Drivers: Declining Farmer Exchange Rate (NTP) from 99.55 (2015) to 93.44 (2019) in Konawe; low cropping intensity (137% in Pelosika without project); high yield gap (current productivity ~4.1–4.4 t/ha paddy vs. potential 7–8 t/ha).

2. Scope

📍 Pelosika Irrigation Complex (Konawe)
  • Area: 20,050 Ha (existing 18,086 Ha + new 1,964 Ha)
  • Beneficiaries: ~27,500 farm households
  • Reservoir capacity: 822.26 MCM from Pelosika Dam
  • Includes DI Wawotobi, Walay, Alowu
📍 Rongkong Irrigation Scheme (North Luwu)
  • Area: 27,695 Ha (existing 10,544 Ha + new 17,151 Ha)
  • Beneficiaries: ~26,900 farm households in DI
  • Water from new Baliase Weir
  • Mix of existing rice fields, plantations, shrubs

Both projects include master planning, feasibility studies, detailed engineering design (DED), AMDAL, LARP, and social protection frameworks.

3. Objectives

  • Increase rural income and enhance food security through improved irrigation infrastructure.
  • Raise cropping intensity (IP) from ~137% to 230% (Pelosika) and from 184% to 210% (Rongkong).
  • Boost paddy productivity from 4.1–4.4 t/ha to 7.6–8.0 t/ha in mature phase.
  • Strengthen institutional capacity of Water User Associations (P3A/GP3A) and farmer groups.
  • Reduce poverty and improve farmer welfare (increase net revenue per farm household).

4. Methodology

The analysis followed ADB’s Economic and Financial Assessment (EFA) framework using an Excel-based model. Key approaches:

  • Economic indicators scoring (GRDP contribution, growth rate, income per capita, fiscal capacity) to determine development scenario (Optimistic, Moderate, Pessimistic, Severe, Very Severe) → Both sites resulted in MODERATE SCENARIO.
  • Cost-benefit analysis over 25-year project life (3–5 years construction, 9% discount rate).
  • Shadow wage factor (0.8) and standard conversion factors (tradable 1.03, non-tradable 1.00, unskilled labor 0.76).
  • Sensitivity analysis (±10% changes in investment, O&M, input costs, output prices, yield, and 1-year delay).
  • Farm household income projection comparing with-project (W/P) vs. without-project (W/O) scenarios.

6. Outcome

✅ Pelosika (Konawe)
  • ENPV = +Rp 158.4 billion
  • EIRR = 10.60% (above 9% discount rate) → Feasible
  • Net revenue per farm HH: from Rp 10.05 million to Rp 17.21 million (+71%)
  • Net revenue per ha: from Rp 19.1 million to Rp 22.4 million
✅ Rongkong (North Luwu)
  • ENPV = +Rp 575.2 billion
  • EIRR = 11.51% → Feasible
  • Net revenue per farm HH: from Rp 6.25 million to Rp 46.42 million (+642%)
  • Net revenue per ha: from Rp 7.0 million to Rp 18.4 million

Sensitivity analysis shows robustness: Pelosika remains feasible under +10% investment cost, +10% O&M, and 1-year delay, but fails under -10% crop prices or -10% yield. Rongkong stays feasible except under severe price drop (-10% crop price).

7. Impact

  • Economic: Increased GRDP contribution from agriculture (28–47% of regional economy), higher per capita income, reduced poverty (Pelosika: 16%→12%; Rongkong: 13.9%→13.6%).
  • Social: Improved farmer welfare (NTP stabilizes), reduced rural unemployment, better human development index (HDI rises to >71).
  • Environmental: Climate-smart water management, promotion of sustainable pesticide use, avoidance of involuntary resettlement (LARP ensures livelihood restoration).
  • Food security: National rice self-sufficiency strengthened, reduced import dependency.

8. Sustainability

🔧 Financial & Institutional Sustainability
  • Government fiscal capacity: Konawe (score 0.56 – moderate; 2019: 1.024 high); North Luwu (0.65 – moderate). Both above provincial averages → able to cover O&M costs (total O&M Pelosika Rp 118.5 billion over 25 years; Rongkong Rp 248.3 billion).
  • Farmer contribution via water service fees and cooperative models (irrigation cooperatives, pump irrigation enterprises).
  • Entrepreneurship potential: tourism, sediment bricks, aquaponics, drip irrigation for horticulture.
  • Periodic O&M scheduled every 6 years to prevent asset deterioration.

9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

📌 Key Lessons

  • Moderate economic scenarios require partial subsidies (e.g., fertilizers, pesticides) and strong extension services to achieve EIRR >15%.
  • Low initial farmer exchange rate (NTP) indicates production costs rose faster than output prices — need for price stabilization and input efficiency.
  • Longer land development periods (10 years for Rongkong) require phased budgeting and political commitment.
  • Without institutional strengthening (P3A, GP3A, cooperatives), yield gains alone do not guarantee poverty reduction.

📋 Policy Recommendations

  • Subsidy redesign: Target smart subsidies (e.g., NPK, bio-pesticides) rather than blanket urea subsidy; link to adoption of climate-smart agriculture.
  • Local regulation (Perda): Enact Perda on irrigation asset management, farmer capacity building, and agribusiness partnerships (DTPHP-P3A-Gapoktan-BUMDes).
  • Multi-year budgeting: APBN and APBD commitment to O&M (at least 1.5% annual routine + 7% periodic) to avoid infrastructure deterioration.
  • Farmer entrepreneurship: Establish irrigation cooperatives for input supply, machinery rental, and output marketing; promote non-farm water uses (fish ponds, water spinach).
  • Monitoring & evaluation: Use economic indicators (GRDP contribution, NTP, fiscal capacity) biennially to reassess scenario and adapt action plans.

10. Brief Policy Note

📢 For the Ministry of Public Works and Regional Governments:

The Pelosika and Rongkong irrigation projects are economically feasible (EIRR 10.6% and 11.5% respectively) and socially beneficial. To maximize returns, policies should focus on: (i) ensuring cost recovery through progressive water fees, (ii) embedding climate resilience into design (e.g., automated weather stations), (iii) accelerating land development with community participation, and (iv) linking irrigated agriculture to off-farm enterprises. Fiscal capacity is adequate; however, delays in O&M funding would erode benefits. A National Irrigation Modernization Roadmap incorporating these lessons is recommended for replication across other DI in Indonesia.
