💧 Maninjau Lake Strategic Implementation Plan

Economic & Financial Analysis (Optimistic Scenario) | Integrated Water Resources Management

ADB – ESP Priority Lake (West Region) | Ministry of Public Works & Housing

1️⃣ Background & Urgency

Lake Maninjau (West Sumatra) is a volcanic lake covering 9,737 ha, under severe environmental stress: sedimentation, eutrophication, massive floating net aquaculture (KJA), land-use change, and water quality deterioration. These pressures threaten a 64 MW hydropower plant, fisheries (IDR 112 billion/year economic loss), tourism, and drinking water for ~35,783 people. The lake is listed as a National Priority Lake under Indonesia’s RPJMN 2020–2024. Without an integrated project, annual flood damages, agricultural losses, and KJA losses will escalate, undermining regional economy.

📌 Scope of Work

Catchment area in Tanjung Raya District (9 nagari). Five sub-projects: (1) Conservation & reforestation; (2) Water resources utilization (groundsills); (3) Damage control – river normalization, communal WWTP, flood mitigation; (4) Integrated lake information & early warning system; (5) Community empowerment & institutional strengthening. Project life: 20 years (2023–2042), construction 2023–2025, full operation from 2026.

🎯 Objectives

  • Develop a strategic implementation plan for sustainable water resources management in Maninjau Lake.
  • Assess economic & financial feasibility of an integrated lake rescue program (conservation, flood control, livelihood improvement).
  • Enhance lake water quality, reduce disaster risk, and increase farmers/fishermen/tourism income.
  • Establish a robust institutional framework and community-based monitoring.

⚙️ Methodology

Economic and Financial Analysis (EFA) following ADB Guidelines (1997, 2014). “Without project” vs “with project” comparison using standard ADB spreadsheet model. Discount rate: 9% (economic opportunity cost of capital). Standard Conversion Factor = 0.99; Shadow wage rate = 0.76 for unskilled labor. Benefits measured as avoided damages (buildings, agriculture, KJA, industry) + additional tourism revenue + environmental improvements. Sensitivity tests: +10% investment cost, +10% O&M, one-year delay.

📦 Outputs

  • Integrated Strategic Plan + DED for 5 sub-projects.
  • Economic & Financial Analysis report with ENPV, EIRR, EBCR.
  • 20-year financing matrix (economic investment cost: IDR 491.5 billion; financial cost: IDR 564 billion).
  • Lake operation model, unified water information system, and institutional design.

Direct benefits: Avoided building damage (IDR 261M), avoided agricultural losses (IDR 4.9B), avoided KJA losses (IDR 183.4B). Tourism revenue jumps from IDR 764B to IDR 2,077B (optimistic scenario). Farmers’ net revenue increase of IDR 13.97 million/ha/year. Flood protection for 104 buildings and 9,654 ha of agricultural land (Q100).

🌏 Impact (Long-term)

  • Environmental: Reduced sedimentation, improved water quality, conservation of endemic species (pensi, palai rinuak).
  • Socio-economic: Income uplift for 5,574 farmer/fisherfolk households; creation of 339 new tourism-related jobs and 51 new SMEs.
  • Resilience: Reliable raw water supply, integrated early warning for floods and extreme events.
  • National contribution: Direct support to SDG 6, 13, 15 and RPJMN 2020–2024 priority lakes program.

♻️ Sustainability

Funding through ADB loan + APBN co-financing. Annual O&M (2.5% of investment) and periodic O&M (7.5% every 5 years). Institutional sustainability: Establishment of Lake Management Agency, “Green Maninjau Movement” with community-based monitoring, PPP for WWTP (KPBU), training on eco-farming, 5R waste management, and ecotourism certification. Local government commitment embedded in regional spatial plan.

📚 Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

  • Key lessons: Integrated catchment approach (conservation + structural + economic incentives) yields higher resilience; community readiness and inclusive training are decisive for long-term success; optimistic tourism growth (150% post-construction) requires aggressive promotion and destination management.
  • Policy recommendations:
    • ✅ Central government should provide a dedicated grant for the establishment of a permanent lake management institution at district level.
    • ✅ Promote PPP schemes for communal WWTP and sustainable water tariffs.
    • ✅ Enforce carrying-capacity based zoning for KJA and phase out illegal nets.
    • ✅ Integrate the lake information system (real-time water level, rainfall, early warning) into regional disaster management agency (BPBD).
    • ✅ Launch a “Green Maninjau Ecotourism” certification to ensure environmental and social safeguards.

📋 Policy Brief (Executive Summary)

Verdict: The Maninjau Lake Rescue Project is economically viable (EIRR 10.69% > discount rate, ENPV positive) and generates large non-market benefits. The initial financial investment of IDR 564 billion yields avoided damages of IDR 237 billion in KJA sector alone, and doubles tourism revenue. Government is recommended to fast-track communal WWTP, rehabilitate critical lands in the catchment, formalize a multi-stakeholder lake board, and launch community-based ecotourism as a green economic engine.
