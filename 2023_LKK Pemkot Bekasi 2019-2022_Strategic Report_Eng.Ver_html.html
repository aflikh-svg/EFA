Executive Summary
Bekasi City Regional Financial Performance Analysis

Fiscal Years 2019–2022 · Prepared by BAPPELITBANGDA Bekasi City

Strategic Report – 2023
1. Background & Urgency

Evaluating Bekasi City’s financial management is essential to assess local government capacity in funding administration, development, and public services. During 2019–2022, macroeconomic shocks (COVID-19 pandemic, budget refocusing) caused significant fluctuations in revenue and expenditure. This report measures fiscal autonomy, efficiency, and decentralisation ratios to guide more resilient fiscal policies.

Urgency: High reliance on transfer funds (average 54.88% of total revenue) and elevated operational spending (79.33% of total expenditure) require strategic intervention to boost Local Own-Source Revenue (PAD) and enhance spending effectiveness.

2. Scope of Work
  • Analysis of APBD performance 2019–2022 (revenue, expenditure, financing).
  • Comparison of budget targets vs. actual realisations.
  • Computation of financial ratios: fiscal independence, efficiency, fiscal decentralisation (DDF), variance analysis.
  • Review of regional balance sheet and fiscal capacity.
  • Alignment of development programme indicators with budget realisation.
3. Objectives
  1. To produce a regional development analysis focused on financial performance based on prior years’ revenue, expenditure, and financing.
  2. To deliver policy recommendations for improving fiscal autonomy and budget efficiency.
  3. To provide a supporting document for better APBD governance in Bekasi City.
4. Methodology

Mixed-method approach: quantitative (descriptive statistics, financial ratios, variance analysis, Location Quotient, Shift-Share) and qualitative (document review, stakeholder discussions). Data sources: BPKAD Bekasi City (budget realisation reports, balance sheets 2019-2022) and BPS. Key indicators: Independence Ratio, Expenditure Efficiency, Fiscal Decentralisation Degree (DDF), Natural/Human Resources Potential, and fiscal capacity projections 2023–2027.

Methods: Variance analysis · Liquidity/Solvency Ratios · Location Quotient · Shift-Share

5. Output
  • Comprehensive financial performance analysis document for Bekasi City.
  • Matrix of financial ratios (2019–2022) and revenue/expenditure projections 2023-2027.
  • Identification of 11 economic base sectors (LQ>1) – Electricity & Gas, Transportation, Health Services, Trade, etc.
  • Strategic fiscal and economic policy recommendations.
6. Outcome
  • Enhanced understanding of gaps between revenue targets and actual performance.
  • Strengthened evidence base for RKPD and RPJMD formulation.
  • Actionable direction to optimise PAD (44.66% of 2022 revenue) and improve operational spending efficiency (budget execution rate 82–88%).
7. Impact
  • Improved budget planning credibility and reduced negative variance.
  • Increase in fiscal independence from average 42.48% PAD contribution to projected >45% by 2025.
  • More targeted capital expenditure (currently 19.4% of total spending).
  • Stronger local fiscal resilience against external shocks (pandemic / recession).
8. Sustainability

This analysis is embedded into the annual regional development planning cycle (RKPD) and the 2025-2029 RPJMD. Recommendations include quarterly financial ratio monitoring and the implementation of a regional financial dashboard. Fiscal capacity projections for 2023–2027 indicate potential surplus that can be channelled into BUMD equity participation and priority infrastructure. Continuous evaluation is crucial due to evolving central transfer policies and national fiscal dynamics.

9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations
📌 Key Lessons: The COVID-19 pandemic caused a sharp revenue decline (-14.62% in 2020), yet 2021 rebound proved PAD elasticity. Transfer dependence remains very high (>50%) – calling for local tax/retail innovation. Unexpected expenditure surged 29,438% in 2020 (COVID response), highlighting need for contingency flexibility.

🎯 Strategic Policy Brief – Recommendations:

  • PAD intensification & extensification: Optimise restaurant taxes, BPHTB, land & building tax, and digitise regional levies.
  • Operational efficiency: Personnel expenditure (36.56% of total spending) requires zero-based budgeting review.
  • Boost capital spending (currently only 19.2% of total spending) to drive long-term economic growth.
  • Diversify financing sources: Utilise surplus SILPA (average IDR 839 billion 2019-2022) for productive investments and BUMD synergy.
  • Strengthen fiscal capacity monitoring: Index dropped from “very high” (5.018) to “medium” in 2022; anticipate DAK/DAU allocation changes.
10. Brief Policy Summary

Bekasi City’s Fiscal Decentralisation Degree (DDF) remains at 42.46% (low category) compared to West Java Province (60.68%). Priority policies include: (1) Local tax reform – expanding tax base and tariff adjustment; (2) E-budgeting implementation to raise expenditure efficiency ratio (>85% towards >90%); (3) Capacity building for financial management personnel; (4) Fiscal incentives for leading sectors (manufacturing, transport, trade). Under the “optimistic” economic scenario (3–5% post-pandemic growth), the target independence ratio is set to exceed 50% by 2026.
