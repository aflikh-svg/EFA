Emergency Assistance for Rehabilitation and Reconstruction (EARR)

Project Completion Report (PCR) Summary | ADB Loan No. 3793-INO

Component 1: Public Works Infrastructure (Human Settlements & Water Resources)

1. Background & Urgency

On September 28, 2018, a magnitude 7.4 earthquake struck Central Sulawesi, Indonesia, triggering a devastating tsunami, massive liquefaction, and landslides. The disaster resulted in 2,081 deaths, 1,309 missing, 4,438 injured, and displaced over 200,000 people. Damage and losses reached IDR 24.11 trillion (approx. USD 1.70 billion), severely affecting housing, water supply, irrigation, roads, and coastal protection.

Central Sulawesi’s poverty rate rose from 14.0% to 16.6%. Women and children were disproportionately affected, with over 350,000 women of reproductive age among the displaced. In response, the Government of Indonesia requested ADB’s emergency assistance, leading to the EARR loan of USD 188 million for Component 1 (Public Works Infrastructure).

2. Scope of Project

Output 1

Human Settlement Infrastructure

  • Reconstruction of UIN Palu (Islamic University) – also functions as tsunami shelter.
  • Pasigala Regional Water Supply (SPAM Uveta): WTP Simoro (300 L/s), WTP Oloboju rehabilitation, distribution networks, and 16,486 house connections.
Output 2

Water Resources Infrastructure

  • Gumbasa Irrigation System reconstruction – serving 8,037 ha.
  • Palu Coastal Protection – 7.6 km revetment & 4 boat moorings.
  • Raw water transmission systems (Pasigala, Paneki, Duyu, Tondo) – 55.79 km pipeline.

3. Objectives

Impact: Reduced socioeconomic impact of disasters.
Outcome: Restored access for at least 200,000 affected people (including women and children) to resilient, inclusive, and operational infrastructure.
Target: Ultimately reached 669,682 individuals – exceeding the target by 235%.

4. Methodology

The project used an Emergency Assistance Loan (EAL) modality to fast-track rehabilitation. Implementation followed ADB’s 2017 Procurement Policy, involving open competitive bidding, quality- and cost-based selection for consultants, and community participation (P3A) for tertiary irrigation. The "Build Back Better" (BBB) principle was embedded: seismic-resistant designs, HDPE flexible pipes, SCADA systems, geotextile reinforcement, and elevated structures for tsunami evacuation. Social and environmental safeguards followed ADB SPS (2009) with semi-annual monitoring reports, GRM, and LARPs.

6. Outcome

The project successfully restored access to critical infrastructure. Outcome indicators were surpassed: 669,682 people (338,871 male, 330,811 female) now benefit from resilient water supply, irrigation, coastal protection, and education facilities. Women’s participation in consultations reached 45%–53%, exceeding GAP targets. The Pasigala SPAM Regional (Uveta) now supplies clean water 24/7 to Palu and Sigi regencies, reducing waterborne diseases and time poverty for women.

7. Development Impact

  • Economic: EIRR values for subprojects: UIN Palu (12.04%), Pasigala WTD (14.5%), Coastal Protection (20.55%), Pasigala RWS (25.06%), Gumbasa Irrigation (14.24%) – all above the economic cost of capital.
  • Social: Reduced poverty impact; enhanced agricultural productivity (4.5 → 5.12 ton/ha); 16,486 house connections benefiting ~61,594 individuals.
  • Gender: 8% of local workers were women (target 10% – partially achieved); lactation rooms, separate toilets, and disaster preparedness plans were institutionalized.
  • Disaster resilience: UIN serves as tsunami shelter; HDPE pipes resist liquefaction; coastal revetments reduce abrasion and tidal flooding risks.

8. Sustainability

O&M budgets are allocated via DIPA 2025 for all subprojects. The regional SPAM Uveta is transitioning to a BLUD (Public Service Agency) financial model for operational flexibility. Government commitments include:

  • UIN Palu: Operational funding from Ministry of Religious Affairs.
  • Gumbasa Irrigation: Annual O&M budget from BWSS III (IDR 5-7 billion).
  • Water supply: Revenue from tariffs (IDR 5.11 billion in 2025) plus government subsidies.
  • Capacity building: Training conducted on technical, environmental, social safeguards, and disaster preparedness.

Overall sustainability rating: Likely Sustainable.

9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

🔹 Procurement & Design

  • Ensure DED readiness before tendering; involve external reviewers for critical design (e.g., HDD failures caused delays).
  • Use flexible contract provisions to allow method changes (e.g., open cut → HDD → siphon) without lengthy approvals.

🔹 Land Acquisition & Resettlement

  • Start land acquisition early and secure “clean & clear” status before construction.
  • Strengthen local government coordination for top-up compensation to resolve land disputes.
  • Simplify certificate splitting processes for heirs.

🔹 Livelihood Restoration (LRP)

  • Integrate LRP into regional poverty reduction programs from the start.
  • Assign dedicated technical focal points at district/city levels to monitor LRP budgeting and implementation.

🔹 Institutional & O&M

  • Accelerate BLUD conversion for regional utilities to ensure financial autonomy.
  • Strengthen water user associations (P3A) with capacity building and formal legal recognition.
  • Ensure inter-agency coordination (ADB, World Bank, JICA) to avoid overlaps and optimize asset use.

10. Brief Policy Recommendations

  1. Integrate BBB in all post-disaster projects: Mandate use of liquefaction-resistant materials (HDPE, geotextile) and multi-purpose shelters (schools as evacuation centers).
  2. Enhance safeguard readiness: Front-load land acquisition and LRP planning; establish joint mediation teams with local governments before construction.
  3. Build local capacity: Require contractors to recruit and train local women for skilled positions; provide gender-sensitive facilities at worksites.
  4. Institutionalize cross-lender coordination: Formalize synchronization frameworks for ADB, World Bank, and JICA-financed components to reduce fragmentation.
  5. Sustainable O&M financing: Adopt blended finance (tariff + government subsidy + efficiency gains) for regional water utilities; enforce asset handover protocols before loan closing.
