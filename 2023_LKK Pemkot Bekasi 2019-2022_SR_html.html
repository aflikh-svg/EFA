📊 Regional Financial Performance Analysis

Bekasi City Government | Fiscal Years 2019–2022

Summary Report – English Version

🔍 1. Background & Urgency

The financial management capability of Bekasi City Government is reflected in its APBD (Regional Budget). Evaluating local financial performance is crucial to measure the ability to finance governance, development, and public services. Between 2019–2022, the COVID-19 pandemic caused economic contraction (-2.58% in 2020) and fiscal stress, yet the city showed resilience. Understanding past financial ratios (fiscal independence, efficiency, variance analysis, fiscal decentralization) is urgent to formulate sustainable development strategies and reduce dependency on central transfers.

📌 2. Scope

  • Analysis of revenue, expenditure, and financing performance (2019–2022).
  • Comparison of target vs realization of APBD components.
  • Financial ratio analysis: independence ratio, efficiency, effectiveness of PAD, dependency level, solvency, liquidity.
  • Alignment of program performance indicators with budget realization.
  • Projection of fiscal capacity for 2023–2026.

🎯 3. Objectives

  • To assess regional financial performance based on historical revenue, expenditure, and financing trends.
  • To evaluate the effectiveness and efficiency of budget absorption and PAD collection.
  • To determine strategic economic scenarios and leading sectors.
  • To provide evidence-based policy recommendations for improving fiscal independence and resource allocation.

🧪 4. Methodology

Mixed method: Quantitative (descriptive statistics, ratio analysis, variance analysis, LQ, Shift-Share) and qualitative (literature review, expert consultation). Data sources: BPKAD Bekasi, BPS, LKJ, APBD realization reports 2019–2022.

📈 Key Ratios

Fiscal independence (DDF), Effectiveness of PAD, Dependency ratio (DKD), Efficiency ratio, Solvency & liquidity.

🏭 Sectoral Analysis

Location Quotient (LQ) for base sectors, Shift-Share for competitiveness, economic scenario determination.

📄 5. Output

  • Comprehensive document of Bekasi City’s financial performance analysis (2019–2022).
  • Calculation of 10+ financial ratios and their trends.
  • Identification of 11 base economic sectors (e.g., Electricity & Gas, Transportation, Real Estate, Health Services).
  • Projection of revenue and expenditure 2023–2026.
  • Mapping of program achievements vs budget realization per mission.

📈 6. Outcome

  • Bekasi City’s fiscal independence (DDF) averaged 42.46% (low category, constructive pattern).
  • PAD effectiveness reached 91.7% (moderately effective), with a peak of 101.76% in 2021.
  • Dependency on transfers remained high (average 54.52% — very high category).
  • Operational expenditure (DKR) averaged 78.39%, higher than provincial average.
  • Economic scenario selected: Optimistic (supported by PAD growth, moderate inflation control, and high fiscal capacity index 5.018).
  • Capital expenditure (Belanja Modal) declined from 30% to 19% of total spending over the decade.

🌍 7. Impact

  • Better budget planning and control through evidence-based variance analysis.
  • Improved prioritization of infrastructure and social programs based on sectoral competitiveness (11 base sectors identified).
  • Strengthened local revenue strategies (e.g., optimizing restaurant taxes, BPHTB, PBB).
  • Reduced unabsorbed budget (SiLPA) from 492% to 99% of financing, indicating improved execution.
  • Policy dialogue enhanced between executive and legislative on fiscal sustainability.

♻️ 8. Sustainability

  • Recommendations integrated into RPJMD and annual RKPD planning cycles.
  • Continuous monitoring of PAD intensification and tax digitization.
  • Capacity building for SKPD in budget formulation and performance-based budgeting.
  • Renewal of analysis every 3–5 years to track fiscal decentralization progress.

📚 9. Lesson Learned for Policy Recommendations

  • Over-reliance on transfers: despite increased PAD, dependency on central funds remains very high (>50%). Need for aggressive local tax base expansion.
  • Effectiveness fluctuates: PAD target setting in 2019 was too optimistic (72.99% achievement). Lesson: use rolling average based on 3-year realization.
  • Low capital spending: declining belanja modal hampers infrastructure renewal. Recommendation: mandate minimum 25% of budget for capital expenditure.
  • SiLPA management: large SiLPA indicates under-spending; improve project readiness and disbursement monitoring.
  • Economic resilience: pandemic showed vulnerability in service sectors; diversify to manufacturing and digital economy.

📋 10. Brief Policy Recommendations

  • Fiscal Independence: Expand PAD by digitalizing tax collection (restaurant, hotel, parking) and reforming retribution tariffs.
  • Efficiency in Spending: Apply zero-based budgeting for operational expenditures, redirect savings to infrastructure.
  • Capital Investment: Allocate at least 25% of APBD to belanja modal, focusing on high-LQ sectors (Transportation, Health, Trade).
  • Debt & Risk Management: Limit new borrowing to productive infrastructure with clear revenue streams.
  • Performance Alignment: Strengthen the "sandingan" (alignment) between program indicators and budget outcomes through e-planning tools.
  • Disaster & Contingency: Maintain a flexible contingency fund (2–3% of total budget) for future shocks.
