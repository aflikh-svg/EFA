Economic & Financial Analysis Summary

Integrated Strategic Plan for Sustainable Water Resources Management
Priority Lake – Toba (North Sumatra)

ESP Project – ADB Funding | Final Report: 2022

1. Background & Urgency

Indonesia's National Medium-Term Development Plan (RPJMN 2020-2024) prioritizes water security and lake sustainability. Lake Toba, a volcanic supervolcano caldera covering 1,130 km² with a catchment area of 385,597 hectares, faces severe pressures: land degradation, erosion, water pollution from domestic/industrial waste, and declining water quality. The lake supports 7 districts, over 2.6 million people, agriculture, fisheries, and tourism. Uncoordinated spatial planning has led to increased flood risk, habitat loss, and economic losses. Without immediate integrated intervention, ecosystem services and local livelihoods will deteriorate further. The government, supported by ADB’s Engineering Services Project (ESP), mandated a strategic implementation plan to reverse this trend.

2. Scope of Work

The project covers the entire Lake Toba catchment area across seven regencies: Karo, Simalungun, Toba Samosir, Tapanuli Utara, Humbang Hasundutan, Samosir, and Dairi. The assessment includes economic and financial feasibility of five sub-projects: (1) Conservation (reforestation, check dams, biodiversity protection); (2) Water Resources Utilization (domestic water, irrigation, micro-hydro); (3) Damage Control (river normalization, communal WWTP); (4) Empowerment & Supervision (institutional strengthening, eco-farming, waste management); (5) Water Resources Information (early warning system, climate data). The evaluation period spans 20 years (2023–2042) with construction from 2023 to 2025.

3. Objectives

  • To assess the economic viability (ENPV, EIRR, EBCR) of the integrated water resources management plan.
  • To evaluate financial sustainability from the perspective of direct beneficiaries (farmers, tourism SMEs, households).
  • To quantify direct and indirect benefits: reduced flooding losses, increased agricultural productivity, improved water quality, enhanced tourism revenues.
  • To provide recommendations for policy alignment and institutional strengthening for sustainable lake governance.

4. Methodology

The analysis follows ADB’s Guidelines for Economic Analysis of Projects (1997, 2014) and the standard EFA spreadsheet model (ADB Indonesia Resident Mission training, 2020). A “with project” vs. “without project” scenario approach is used. Key assumptions: 9% discount rate (opportunity cost of capital); economic life 20 years; standard conversion factors (SCF=0.99; unskilled labor SWF=0.76); 11% VAT removed from economic costs. Financial costs (Rp 1,550.4 billion investment) are converted to economic costs (Rp 1,367.8 billion). Benefits include avoided damages (buildings, agriculture, fishponds, industry) and incremental tourism income. Sensitivity analysis tests variations in investment cost (+10%), O&M (+10%), damage levels (+10%), and project delay (1 year).

5. Outputs (Deliverables)

  • Detailed investment cost estimates per sub-project (total financial investment IDR 1,550.4 billion; economic value IDR 1,367.8 billion).
  • Routine O&M (2.5% of civil works/year) and periodic O&M (7.5% every 5 years).
  • Economic indicator results: ENPV = IDR 631.71 billion; EIRR = 26.94%; EBCR = 2.12.
  • Sensitivity analysis tables showing robustness under adverse scenarios.
  • Identification of beneficiaries: 2.65 million people, 782,630 households, 624,850 farmers, 430,072 entrepreneurs, and 1.9 million annual tourists by 2026.

6. Outcomes (Expected)

  • Reduction in annual economic losses: building damage reduced from IDR 2.78 trillion to IDR 1.23 trillion; agricultural losses from IDR 95 billion to IDR 11.5 billion.
  • Improved water quality in Lake Toba leading to healthier aquatic biodiversity (preservation of endemic fish Rasbora tobana and Neolissochilus thienemanni).
  • Increase in tourist arrivals from 1.70 million (2020) to 1.90 million by 2026, with 10% growth in the first year of operation.
  • Creation of ~1,900 direct jobs in tourism support services (guides, parking, cleaning, homestays, F&B).
  • Strengthened community institutions for lake conservation and early warning systems for extreme weather.

7. Impact (Long-term)

Alignment with SDG 6 (clean water), SDG 8 (decent work & economic growth), SDG 13 (climate action), and SDG 15 (life on land). Contributes to Indonesia’s RPJPN 2005-2025 and ADB Country Partnership Strategy 2020-2024. Enhanced resilience of the Lake Toba catchment to climate-induced floods and droughts. Sustainable tourism development based on the vision: "Tourism, building and advancing ancestral land, by and for all communities". Reduction in national poverty indicators through improved agricultural productivity and alternative livelihoods.

8. Sustainability

Financial sustainability is ensured through: (i) routine O&M budget allocated from national/regional government; (ii) potential PPP scheme for communal WWTP operation involving local water utilities; (iii) local retribution from parking, entrance tickets, and tourism services (willingness-to-pay: entry IDR 15,000, car parking IDR 20,000/day). Social sustainability is anchored in community empowerment and training (eco-farming, 5R waste management, ecotourism). Environmental sustainability relies on reforestation, gully plugs, check dams, and biodiversity protection. Institutional sustainability requires continuous mentoring by local governments and active community groups (environmental care groups).

9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

Lessons: Integrated water management requires simultaneous action on conservation, utilization, and damage control. Economic analysis at planning stage identifies high returns (EIRR >20%) but only if institutional coordination is in place. Delays in project start significantly reduce ENPV (switching value -222% for investment cost).

Policy Recommendations:

  • Adopt the Economic and Financial Analysis as the basis for ADB loan approval and accelerate sub-project implementation.
  • Enforce regulations on groundwater extraction (revise PP No. 43/2008) and promote surface water pricing based on MPWH Decree No. 12/KPTS/M/2019.
  • Strengthen regional capacity for O&M through dedicated lake basin organizations (BWS) and participatory monitoring.
  • Develop tourism carrying capacity strategies to avoid over-exploitation while maximizing local employment.
  • Integrate early warning systems for extreme weather into regional disaster management plans.

10. Brief Policy Note

Executive Summary for Policymakers: The Lake Toba Strategic Water Resources Plan is economically viable (ENPV +Rp632 billion, EIRR 27%, BCR 2.1) and financially sustainable. Total investment cost IDR 1.55 trillion (economic cost IDR 1.37 trillion) yields high socioeconomic returns: avoiding IDR 1.5 trillion in flood damages, boosting agricultural output, and increasing tourism revenues. For ADB and Ministry of Public Works, immediate approval and disbursement are recommended. Key policy actions: finalize institutional framework for lake management, accelerate communal WWTP construction under PPP, and mandate local government co-financing for routine O&M. The project directly supports national water security targets and the Lake Toba tourism super-priority destination.
