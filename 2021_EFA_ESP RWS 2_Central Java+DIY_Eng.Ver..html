📊 Economic & Financial Summary Report

Raw Water Supply Intake & Transmission (Package-2)

ADB Loan No. 3455-INO | Central Java & DI Yogyakarta

1. Background & Urgency

The Government of Indonesia, through DGWR-MPWH, aims to enhance water security in Central Java. Growing domestic, industrial, and urban demands outpace existing raw water capacity. The Dadi Muria weir, Jragung Dam, and Bener Dam projects are strategic to serve 1.2+ million people across 10 districts. Without intervention, water scarcity will hinder public health, economic productivity, and regional equity.

2. Scope of Work

  • Dadi Muria System (Klambu Weir) – 500 Lps → Pati, Kudus, Demak, Grobogan.
  • Jragung Dam System – 1,000 Lps → Semarang City, Demak, Grobogan.
  • Bener Dam System – 1,500 Lps → Kebumen, Purworejo, Kulon Progo.
  • Construction of intakes, transmission pipelines (total ~256 km), WTP integration, and PPP readiness.

3. Objectives

To assess economic & financial viability (ENPV, EIRR, BCR) of three raw water schemes, determine minimum feasible raw water tariffs, and propose sustainable implementation models (incl. PPP/BOT) ensuring full cost recovery while maintaining affordability.

4. Methodology

Based on ADB’s 1997/2014 Guidelines and EFA tool (Jakarta training, Mar 2020). Key steps:

  • Discount rate 12% (opportunity cost of capital, Indonesia); evaluation 50 years (2026–2072).
  • Shadow exchange rate factor (SERF=1.01) and shadow wage factor (SWF=0.76) derived from 2020 trade data.
  • Surface water acquisition value (SWAV) calculated per MPWH Reg. 15/2017, 18/2015 & Kepmen 12/2019.
  • Sensitivity tests: +10% O&M, +10% investment, tariff delays.
  • Willingness to pay (WTP) analysis from PDAMs in Central Java.

5. Output (Economic Results)

🏞️ Dadi Muria EIRR 13.22%
ENPV +3,741 mil IDR
Min tariff: IDR 1,390/m³ → final: 1,425/m³
🌊 Jragung Dam EIRR 12.26%
ENPV +8,663 mil IDR
Min tariff: IDR 6,757/m³ → final: 6,798/m³
🏔️ Bener Dam EIRR 12.4%
ENPV +9,150 mil IDR
Min tariffs: Purworejo 889, Kebumen 2,441, Kulon Progo 2,797 IDR/m³

Sensitivity: All projects remain viable (EIRR >9% ADB, >12% MPWH) under +10% cost overruns; Jragung shows higher vulnerability but still above threshold.

6. Outcome

Three RWS sub‑projects are economically feasible and financially sustainable if raw water tariffs are set at full cost recovery levels (FCR). PDAMs’ willingness to pay ranges IDR 125–2,400/m³; therefore subsidies or cross‑subsidies will be required for Jragung and Kebumen/Kulon Progo segments.

7. Impact

  • Social: Access to reliable raw water for ~1.24 million people (200k Dadi Muria + 230k Jragung + 805k Bener).
  • Economic: Reduced waterborne diseases, support to local industry & tourism (YIA airport), increased agricultural resilience.
  • Environmental: Efficient use of dam releases, river maintenance flow preserved (0.15–7.52 m³/s).

8. Sustainability

Institutional: Proposed PPP via BOT/ROT schemes with PDAM/PDAB as PJPK (PP 122/2015). Regional governments provide financial/non-financial support.
Financial: O&M funded by tariff revenue; periodic renewal (10% of construction cost every 5 years) included.
Technical: 50-year economic life with standard pipeline/WTP design, BMKG/climate resilience considered.

9. Lesson Learned & Policy Recommendations

  • Tariff gap: MPWH-regulated SWAV (IDR 104–295/m³) is far below FCR (IDR 889–6,757/m³). A national raw water pricing reform is urgent.
  • Cross‑subsidies: Industrial/commercial users (factor up to 12.5) must bear higher tariffs to protect household affordability.
  • Long‑term O&M: Escrow accounts for periodic renewal should be mandated.
  • PPP de‑risking: Government guarantees for take‑or‑pay and demand risk sharing to attract private capital.
  • Data harmonisation: Standardised EFA tools across provinces to improve comparability and decision speed.
