🌿 Konawe Priority Area Masterplan

Integrated Development & Spatial Planning for Industrial Impact Zones | Final Report Summary

National Strategic Project | Sustainable Industrial Corridor

1. Background & Urgency

The Indonesian Government, following the President’s directive (Rakornas Jan 2023), emphasizes well-planned regional development to avoid uncontrolled urban sprawl and slum enclaves. The Ministry of PPN/Bappenas identified 23 priority regencies/cities requiring immediate intervention. Konawe Regency is selected due to the rapid expansion of the Konawe Industrial Estate (KI Konawe) – a National Strategic Project (PSN) for nickel downstreaming (smelters). Uncontrolled migration, environmental pressures, infrastructure gaps, and potential social conflicts demand a detailed masterplan to mitigate negative impacts while maximizing economic opportunities.

2. Scope of the Masterplan

The planning area covers the directly affected zones around KI Konawe, spanning across 9 districts within Konawe Regency: Morosi, Bondoala, Kapoiala, Pondidaha, Besulutu, Sampara, Anggolomoare, Lalonggasumeeto, and Soropia. It distinguishes between "core zone" (immediate industrial vicinity) and "buffer zone" (surrounding supporting areas). The masterplan integrates macro, meso, and micro spatial scales.

3. Objectives

🎯 General Objective Provide actionable recommendations for development and spatial restructuring to resolve regional issues and accelerate sustainable growth in priority areas (including Konawe).
📌 Specific Target Produce a comprehensive masterplan that serves as a binding guideline for central agencies, local governments, private sector, and communities in planning, constructing, and organizing the industrial-affected territory.

4. Methodology

Multi-pronged approach combining:

  • Exploratory & Participatory – FGDs, stakeholder mapping, and collaborative workshops.
  • Yuridis/Normative & Empirical – Review of spatial plans (RTRW, RDTR), environmental permits, and field surveys.
  • THIS approach (Tematik, Holistik, Integratif, Spasial) and scenario-based phasing (consolidation, development, stabilization, expansion).
  • Urban design & sustainability frameworks – sponge city, green infrastructure, multi-sector impact analysis (labor, housing, environment, economy).

5. Key Outputs

🗺️ Spatial & Infrastructure Plans
Protected area zoning, industrial allocation, settlement patterns, clean water & sanitation schemes, drainage, solid waste management, and transport networks (roads, bridges, public transport).
🏭 City Branding & Vision
“Pusat Agrowisata dan Kota Industri” (Agrotourism & Industrial City). Vision: "Konawe as an integrated, consolidated, and self-reliant agrotourism and industrial region".
📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Measurable targets: 100% clean water coverage, wastewater treatment expansion, TPS3R facilities, poverty reduction to 10.91% by 2044, IPM increase to 78.67, etc.
🖼️ Urban Design & Quick Wins
Visual illustrations: iconic bridge as entrance gate, sponge city (oxbow lake revitalization), public wetlands, green pedestrian promenades, vertical housing (Rusunawa) near commercial hubs.

6. Expected Outcomes

  • Integrated spatial arrangement: separation between industrial zones, settlements, and protected areas (buffer forests, mangrove belts).
  • Improved basic infrastructure: drinking water coverage (from low baseline to >90% by 2043), communal & centralized domestic wastewater treatment (SPALD-T), and modern waste management reducing open dumping.
  • Enhanced connectivity: new road corridors (Abelisawah–Tabanggele bridge, Pebunooha, Paku Jaya–Morosi) and ferry terminals reduce logistics costs and travel time.
  • Strengthened local economy: UMKM clusters, agro-tourism packages (KALOSARA culture integration), fish landing ports, and agriculture support.

7. Anticipated Impact

  • Economic: Creation of ~504,200 jobs at full industrial capacity (phased by 2043); higher local revenue and downstream nickel value chain (batteries, stainless steel).
  • Social: Reduced informal settlements, better public health (reduced ISPA, stunting), cultural resilience (Tolaki’s KALOSARA philosophy as social glue).
  • Environmental: Continuous water quality monitoring (SPARING), reforestation (4,500 trees already planted), mangrove rehabilitation, and sponge city concept to mitigate flooding.
  • Spatial: Controlled land conversion, protected paddy fields (LP2B policy), and greenbelts between industry and housing.

8. Sustainability Strategy

Institutional & financial: Establishment of UPT SPAM, UPT waste management, PPP schemes, CSR from PT VDNI and other industries. Phasing: 4 five-year stages (Consolidation → Development → Strengthening → Expansion) from 2025–2044. Green industry: low-emission technology, waste-to-energy potential, and eco-drainage. Community empowerment: training for local workers (6% local labor quota rising to higher share), support for BUMDes and cooperatives.

9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

📌 Key lessons: Rapid industrial growth without proactive spatial correction leads to congestion, environmental degradation, and informal housing. Strong multi-level coordination (national, provincial, district) is crucial; early infrastructure investment (water, sanitation, roads) must match labour migration speed.

✅ Policy recommendations:
• Integrate masterplan as amendment to RDTR and RTRW – enforce strict zoning for industry–settlement buffer distance (min 2 km).
• Mandate AMDAL and continuous online wastewater monitoring (SPARING) for all smelter operations.
• Accelerate land acquisition and funding for SPAM pipelines and IPAL communal to avoid reliance on groundwater.
• Promote agro-tourism and sustainable fisheries as post-mining economic base – “KALOSARA” cultural branding.
• Provide incentives (tax, licensing) for green industries and developers building affordable vertical housing for workers.
• Establish a special coordinating body (KI Konawe Authority) for spatial enforcement and CSR synergy.

10. Brief Policy Extract

The masterplan recommends immediate implementation of quick wins (2024–2025): bridge construction Abelisawah–Tabanggele, pedestrian pathways in Morosi/Bondoala, TPS3R units, and legal protection of LP2B. Mid-term policies involve large-scale SPAM network expansion, construction of intermediate waste transfer stations (SPA), and finalizing the industrial city branding. Long-term policies aim for a self-reliant region where nickel revenues finance ecotourism and agro-industry, ensuring transition after mine lifecycle.
