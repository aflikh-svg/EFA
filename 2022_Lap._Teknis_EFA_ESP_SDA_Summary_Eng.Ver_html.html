📋 SUMMARY REPORT: ECONOMIC AND FINANCIAL ANALYSIS

Engineering Services Project (ESP) – DGWR, Ministry of PUPR, Indonesia | ADB Loan No. 3455 INO

Period: 2016 – 2023 | Final Evaluation based on Technical Report (Dec 2022)

📌 1. Background & Urgency

Indonesia’s infrastructure gap has hindered inclusive growth due to underinvestment, weak project preparation, complex land acquisition, and limited implementation capacity. The Engineering Services Project (ESP) was designed as the first "umbrella" project to massively improve the quality and timeliness of feasibility studies, detailed engineering designs (DED), environmental assessments, and land acquisition for strategic infrastructure. The Directorate General of Water Resources (DGWR) leads 26 work packages covering irrigation, flood management, dams, lakes, and raw water supply. Urgency is driven by the need to accelerate public investment to achieve national food security, disaster resilience, and SDGs 2030.

📐 2. Scope

ESP supports 40 sub-projects across 4 technical directorates within DGWR: (i) Irrigation and Lowland (11 sub-projects), (ii) Rivers and Coastal (8 sub-projects), (iii) Dams and Lakes (9 sub-projects), (iv) Groundwater and Bulk Water (11 sub-projects) plus 1 master plan and 1 PMC. The PMC’s Economic & Financial Analysis (EFA) expert reviewed all economic/financial feasibility outputs, cost estimates, financing plans, and ensured compliance with ADB standards.

🎯 3. Objective

Main objective: Strengthen MPWH’s capacity to efficiently design and implement public investment programs. Specifically: (i) improve project preparation quality leading to timely start of civil works; (ii) enhance institutional capacity in public investment management, including design standards, procurement, and financial analysis. The EFA component aims to ensure all prepared sub-projects are economically viable, financially sustainable, and aligned with national priorities (RPJMN 2020-2024, NAWACITA).

⚙️ 4. Methodology

EFA methodology follows ADB guidelines using standard templates (ENPV, EIRR, BCR with 9% discount rate). The PMC monitored, assisted, and evaluated PPC (Project Preparation Consultant) outputs. Performance evaluation for the Project Completion Report (PCR) was based on relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, and sustainability (4 criteria, each 25% weight). Data sources: final PPC reports, ADB EFA tools, contract status, multiplier effect analysis, and downstream financing mapping.

Key tools: Economic NPV, EIRR, BCR; Multiplier effect (investment leverage); O&M cost estimation; financing source mapping (APBN, ADB, World Bank, PPP).

📦 5. Output

  • 38 sub-projects with completed economic & financial analysis (24 packages evaluated).
  • Total estimated new investment value: IDR 152.1 trillion (approx. USD 10.5 billion).
  • Total PPC contract value: IDR 1.15 trillion → multiplier effect 132x (irrigation: 65x; flood: 96x; dams/lakes: 56x; raw water: 136x).
  • All sub-projects show positive ENPV, EIRR above 9% (averages 15.99% – 17.99%), and BCR >1 (1.5 – 2.21).
  • Downstream financing secured: APBN (10 projects), ADB (25.5 projects), World Bank (4.5 projects), PPP (1 project).
  • EFA reports delivered for 100% of DILL sub-projects, 62.5% (DRC), 77.78% (DDL), 54.55% (DGWBW). Use of ADB template: 81.8% (DILL), 75% (DRC), 44.44% (DDL), 27.27% (DGWBW).

📈 6. Outcome

  • Improved project quality: Adoption of advanced tech (aerial surveys, digital terrain models, GIS-based asset management, early warning systems).
  • Enhanced institutional capacity in MPWH/DGWR staff through training on EFA, design-and-build contracts, and value engineering.
  • Economic viability confirmed: All sub-projects are economically feasible, contributing to food security, flood damage reduction, raw water availability, and lake ecosystem protection.
  • Performance rating: Overall “Successful” (weighted rating 2.25 out of 3) – Highly Relevant, Effective, Efficient, Likely Sustainable.

🌍 7. Impact

The ESP is expected to support national food security (irrigation expansion & rehabilitation), reduce flood losses (river basin flood management), increase raw water supply for drinking water and industry, and promote sustainable lake management for tourism and ecology. Long-term impacts include better climate resilience, increased farmer incomes, reduced waterborne diseases, and strengthened regional economies via multiplier effects (up to IDR 152 trillion in follow-on investments).

♻️ 8. Sustainability

Sustainability is rated “likely sustainable”. Key factors: (i) downstream financing commitment from APBN, ADB, WB, and PPP; (ii) involvement of Water User Associations (WUAs), BUMDes, tourism awareness groups, and local governments in O&M; (iii) institutional strengthening provided through ESP training. However, risks remain: local government capacity, cost recovery for O&M, and post-project extension support. The project has built ownership among beneficiaries, enhancing long-term viability.

O&M investment: Estimated routine O&M IDR 2.0 trillion/year and periodic O&M IDR 6.0 trillion across all sub-projects – essential for sustainability.

📚 9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations

  • Lesson 1: Early integration of EFA templates – Sub-projects using ADB EFA tools from the start showed higher compliance and faster approval. Policy recommendation: Mandate standardized EFA templates for all water resources projects financed by international loans.
  • Lesson 2: Multiplier effect as advocacy tool – Demonstrating 132x investment leverage helps secure political and fiscal support. Recommendation: Include multiplier effect analysis in all feasibility studies to unlock budget allocations.
  • Lesson 3: O&M funding must be front-loaded – Many projects lacked detailed O&M financing plans at design stage. Recommendation: Require binding O&M agreements with local governments/WUAs before final DED approval.
  • Lesson 4: Downstream financing diversity – Mix of APBN, ADB, WB, and PPP reduces risk. Recommendation: Expand PPP frameworks for raw water supply and dam operations.
  • Lesson 5: Capacity building needs repetition – Only 27-44% of some directorates used ADB templates; continuous training is essential. Recommendation: Establish a permanent EFA unit within DGWR to mentor project preparation teams.

📜 10. Brief Policy

Policy brief summary: The ESP demonstrates that dedicated project preparation facilities significantly improve infrastructure quality. For Indonesia to close its infrastructure gap, the government should (i) replicate the ESP model as a national “Project Preparation Facility” under Ministry of Finance; (ii) enforce use of ADB-aligned EFA tools for all public-private infrastructure; (iii) link O&M budget allocation to project approval; (iv) strengthen local Balai (regional offices) capacities in economic analysis; and (v) mainstream value engineering and climate resilience in all designs. These actions will accelerate SDG achievement and national competitiveness.
