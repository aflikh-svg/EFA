📊 2.1.3 Economy – Socio-Economic Profile (2015–2020)

Project area: Kutai Barat Regency, Kutai Kartanegara Regency, Samarinda City (East Kalimantan Province). Data source: BPS (Central Statistics Agency) for 2015–2020.

1. Poverty Indicators

Poverty line (Rp/cap/month) increased in 2015-2020 for all regions. Percentage of poor people in East Kalimantan was 6.10% in 2020 (↑ from 5.91% in 2019) due to COVID-19. The highest poverty depth (P1) and severity (P2) indices are observed in Kutai Barat and Kutai Kartanegara.

2. Open Unemployment Rate (TPT)

Flactuating but decreasing trend from 2015 to 2020. Unemployment rates in project locations remain above national average but below provincial average.

3. Income Inequality (Gini Ratio)

Gini ratio for East Kalimantan increased from 0.315 (2015) to 0.328 (2020), indicating rising inequality. Samarinda Gini ~0.324, Kutai Barat ~0.338 (2020).

4. Human Development Index (HDI)

All regions show increasing HDI from 2015 to 2020. Samarinda has the highest HDI (above provincial and national average); Kutai Barat and Kutai Kartanegara are below provincial average.

5. Inflation Rate

Inflation decreased across all areas in 2015-2020. Samarinda inflation 2020: 1.76%, East Kalimantan: 1.64%, National: 2.68%.

6. GRDP & Economic Structure

Contribution to East Kalimantan GRDP (2020): Kutai Kartanegara ~24.5%, Samarinda ~8.2%, Kutai Barat ~4.5%.

Economic growth: Negative in 2020 due to pandemic: Kutai Barat -2.92%, Kutai Kartanegara -4.44%, Samarinda -1.07%, Provincial -2.85%.

Main income sources: Samarinda (Construction sector ~21%); Kutai Kartanegara & Kutai Barat (Mining & Quarrying ~45-60% of GRDP).

Per capita income (2019): Kutai Kartanegara Rp 207M, Kutai Barat Rp 177M, Samarinda Rp 78.6M, Province Rp 175.6M.

7. Strategic Sector Determination (Location Quotient – LQ)

Regional (3 areas combined): 15 base sectors (LQ≥1), 2 non-base sectors: Manufacturing (0.277) and Transportation & Warehousing (0.730).

  • Samarinda: 14 base sectors (top: Financial services, Other services, Accommodation). Non-base: Agriculture, Mining, Manufacturing.
  • Kutai Kartanegara: 3 base sectors (Agriculture, Mining, Health services). 14 non-base sectors.
  • Kutai Barat: 7 base sectors (Public administration, Agriculture, Construction, Health, Trade).

8. Shift-Share Analysis (Competitiveness) 2015–2020

Regional aggregate (3 regencies/city): 14 sectors have progressive growth, 3 have negative shift; 11 sectors have competitive advantage (positive differential shift), 6 lack competitiveness.

  • Samarinda: 13 progressive, 4 negative; 7 sectors with competitiveness.
  • Kutai Kartanegara: 15 progressive, 2 negative; 14 sectors have competitiveness.
  • Kutai Barat: 16 progressive, 1 negative; 12 sectors have competitiveness.

9. Regional Fiscal Capacity Map (2015–2019)

Fiscal capacity index: Kutai Kartanegara highest (4.28→3.68), Kutai Barat (6.40→1.59), Samarinda (1.9→2.47). Provincial fiscal capacity declined from 3.66 to 1.22. Categories range from "High" to "Moderate".
