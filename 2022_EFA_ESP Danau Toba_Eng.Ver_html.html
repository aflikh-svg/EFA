Executive Summary

Economic & Financial Analysis – Integrated Strategic Planning for Sustainable Water Resources Management
Lake Toba (National Priority Lake)

ESP Toba Final Report – July 2022
1. Background & Urgency

Lake Toba, one of Indonesia’s 15 national priority lakes (Presidential Regulation No.60/2021), faces severe pressures: sedimentation, pollution, land conversion, and water conflicts. Its catchment area (385,597 ha) supports 2.65 million people across 7 districts, providing hydropower (64 MW), fisheries, and a world-class tourism destination. Without an integrated intervention, environmental degradation will undermine regional economy, water security, and the "Super Priority Destination" status.

⚠️ Urgency: Protect lake ecosystem services, support Indonesia’s RPJMN 2020-2024, SDGs, and ADB Country Partnership Strategy.
2. Scope

The study covers 7 districts within the Lake Toba catchment (Karo, Simalungun, Toba Samosir, North Tapanuli, Humbang Hasundutan, Samosir, Dairi). Five sub-projects are analyzed:

  • Conservation: reforestation, check dams, biodiversity protection.
  • Water resources utilization: groundsill construction, erosion control.
  • Damage control: river normalization, communal WWTP.
  • Water information system: early warning, climate data hub.
  • Empowerment & supervision: eco-farming training, waste management, community-based ecotourism.
3. Objectives
📈 Economic
Boost income of farmers, fishermen, and tourism SMEs via improved environmental quality.
💧 Environmental
Reduce pollution, control floods/landslides, restore water quality.
🏛️ Institutional
Strengthen integrated WRM governance & community capacity.
📊 Investment readiness
Assess economic/financial viability for ADB financing.
4. Methodology

Based on ADB Guidelines for Economic Analysis of Projects and standard EFA spreadsheet model (ADB training March 2020). "With-without project" comparison, 20-year evaluation period (2023–2042), discount rate 12% (opportunity cost of capital). Financial-to-economic conversion uses SCF = 0.99 and shadow wage rate 0.76 for unskilled labor. Sensitivity analysis covers +10% investment cost, +10% O&M, 1-year delay, and +10% damage increase.

5. Outputs
  • Total investment: IDR 2,640.8 billion (financial, gross) / IDR 2,350.9 billion (economic).
  • Routine O&M 2.5% of investment per year; periodic O&M 7.5% every 5 years.
  • Economic feasibility reports for 5 sub-projects with quantified benefits (tourism increase, agricultural productivity, avoided damage).
  • Financial sustainability assessment including willingness-to-pay for tourism services.

6. Outcomes

All three tourism growth scenarios (optimistic, moderate, pessimistic) yield economically viable results (EIRR>12%, ENPV>0, BCR≥1). For the moderate scenario (10% first-year growth, 7% subsequent): EIRR 22.2%, ENPV IDR 265 billion, BCR 1.05. Sensitivity analysis confirms robustness: even with 10% cost overrun or 1-year delay, EIRR remains above 12% (min 19.4%). Financial analysis shows positive cash flow from entrance tickets, parking fees, homestays, and other tourist activities.

7. Impact
🌿 Environmental: Reduced erosion, improved water quality, protection of endemic fish (Rasbora tobana, Batak fish).
👥 Socio-economic: Higher income for 624k farmers+ranchers, creation of ~339 new direct jobs in tourism + multiplier effect.
🏞️ Tourism: Visitor increase from 1.70 million (2020) to 1.90 million by 2026 (moderate scenario). Supports "Green Toba" destination branding.
🏛️ Governance: Strengthened early warning system for hydro-meteorological disasters and integrated lake management institutions.
8. Sustainability

Sustainability is embedded through:

  • Institutional: Central-to-regional grants for O&M; establishment of community environment groups across 7 districts.
  • Financial: PPP potential for WWTP; retention of parking and entry fees for tourism facility maintenance.
  • Social: Continuous training on eco-farming, 5R waste management, and SME business incubation (local governments as facilitators).
📅 Timeline: construction 2023–2025, full operation from 2026, periodic O&M every 5 years (2030,2035,2040) until 2042.
9. Lessons Learned & Policy Recommendations
📌 Key lesson: Integrated water resource management (conservation + utilization + damage control) is far more effective than sectoral interventions. Willingness-to-pay exists when environmental services improve. Main risks: coordination across 7 districts and climate change impacts.

🔹 Recommended policies:

  1. Establish a Lake Toba Management Authority (cross-district/province) with clear mandate and dedicated budget.
  2. Provide fiscal incentives (tax reduction/input subsidy) for farmers adopting eco-farming and reduced chemical fertilizers.
  3. Integrate the water information system (early warning) with spatial planning and green infrastructure investments.
  4. Use the EFA results to apply for ADB sector lending (MFF or results-based) under the ESP framework.
  5. Local governments must allocate at least 10% of special transfer funds for O&M of lake environmental facilities.
10. Brief Policy Summary

For decision-makers: The ESP Lake Toba project is economically and financially viable (EIRR 22.2%, BCR 1.05). Immediate implementation through ADB co-financing and national budget (APBN) is recommended. Key policy actions:

  • Introduce an environmental user fee for tourism operators and large-scale fisheries to generate O&M revenue.
  • Combine reforestation and river normalization with Cash-for-Work programs to absorb local labor.
  • Launch a sub-sovereign green bond to finance communal WWTPs and green infrastructure.
  • Monitor core KPIs: Lake Toba Water Quality Index, tourist arrivals, and average farmer household income.
✅ The project contributes directly to ADB’s Indonesia CPS (2020-2024): sustainable infrastructure, human capital, and economic diversification.
