🌿 Fresh Sehat · Bali Business Plan 2017

Executive summary — integrated healthy meal platform for post-care & preventive nutrition

1. Background & Urgency Context

Indonesia’s health challenges: Uneven access to providers, minimal instant access to nutritionists, expensive post-treatment services, and fragmented healthy food supply. Medical professionals face low BPJS incentives, foreign competition, and geographic barriers. The government struggles with archipelagic distribution, weak health-tech infrastructure, and slow implementation of master plans.

Why urgent: Growing public awareness of healthy living collides with the lack of integrated communication & delivery tech. With 326 million mobile subscriptions, a digital personalized nutrition platform is critical for timely recovery and maintenance.

2. Scope

  • Phase 1 (Year 1): 4 high-HDI regencies/cities in Bali (Denpasar, Badung, Gianyar, Tabanan) – HDI above Bali’s 72.48 and national 68.9.
  • Phase 2: Expansion to neighboring regencies, partnering with at least 2 regional public hospitals (RSUD).
  • Phase 3: Reach underserved areas in Bali.
  • Services: Med Food (medical meals), Health Food (à la carte & subscription), Baby Food, integrated ordering app with online delivery (Grab/Uber/Gojek).

3. Objectives

  • Vision: Deliver fresh, warm, and timely healthy meals through a technology platform that simplifies access to nutrition services.
  • Mission: Become a fast, accurate, and top-tier communication & information platform for healthy nutrition.
  • Target users: Men & women aged 0–80 years, health budget IDR 1.575M – 4.5M/month, across Bali.
  • Guarantee delivery certainty, complete nutritional features, and promo schemes.

4. Methodology

  • Market & SWOT analysis (Strength: hospital network, single pricing; Weakness: limited marketing/IT manpower, remote access; Opportunities: rising HDI & mobile users; Threats: local culinary with nutritionists).
  • Multi-channel promotion: Offline (posters at hospitals, PKK/Posyandu events, portable clinics at Car Free Day, sponsorship) & Online (SEO, Google Adwords, social media campaigns, email/SMS blast).
  • Strategic partnerships: Hospitals (meal production), online transport (Grab/Uber/Gojek for delivery), XL Axiata for IT infrastructure.
  • IT strategy: Dedicated app development team, GPS integration, periodic maintenance and upgrades.
  • Customer retention: Promo rates, full nutrition & health information, periodic therapy updates.

5. Output

📱 Fresh Sehat App (Med Food)
🍱 Med/Health/Baby Food (a la carte & subscription)
🤝 Hospital & Logistics Network
📈 3-year financial projection (P&L, feasibility)
📢 Promotional materials & offline clinics

6. Outcome

  • Integrated digital ecosystem connecting patients, nutritionists, hospitals, and drivers.
  • Increased instant access to therapeutic meals for outpatients & wellness seekers.
  • Partner hospitals generate additional revenue & improve outpatient nutrition adherence.
  • Nutritionists gain direct communication channel with patients beyond hospital walls.
  • Transparent single pricing strategy without hidden costs.

7. Impact

  • Public health: Better post-treatment nutrition adherence, reduced complication risks.
  • Equity: Bridges nutrition service gaps between big cities and remote Balinese regencies via delivery partners.
  • Digital economy: Creates local jobs in IT, marketing, logistics and fosters health-tech startups.
  • Cost efficiency: Reduced readmission rates, potential long-term savings for BPJS and families.

8. Sustainability

  • Recurring revenue model: Revenue sharing with hospitals, food sales margin, in-app ads.
  • Phased roadmap: Year 1 (launch, 4 regencies) → Year 2 (expansion with RSUDs) → Year 3 (remote coverage).
  • Tech continuity: Regular updates, GPS support, dedicated IT division.
  • Long-term partnerships: Ride-hailing revenue sharing after service stabilization, hospital supply contracts.
  • Retention programs: Promos, 24/7 customer service, free nutrition education.

9. Lesson Learned & Policy Recommendations

Key lessons: Digital platforms effectively bridge nutrition access gaps in archipelagic regions. However, limited marketing personnel outside big cities and weak communication infrastructure in remote areas are major bottlenecks.

Policy recommendations:

  • Integrate nutrition apps with BPJS referral and e-medical records.
  • Provide tax incentives/subsidies for health-tech startups serving low-HDI districts.
  • Establish Digital Nutrition Service Centers at every RSUD with dedicated internet budget.
  • Accelerate tele-nutritionist certification and training for remote consultations.
  • Adopt single pricing models for therapeutic meals to protect lower-middle class patients.

10. Brief Policy Summary

Policy title: Acceleration of Integrated Digital Nutrition Services in Archipelagic Regions – Fresh Sehat Bali Model

Quick action points:

  • 📌 National "Tele-Nutrition" program – replicate Fresh Sehat’s platform to ensure post-discharge & community access to clinical meals.
  • 📌 Delivery subsidy for poor patients using BPJS Kapitasi or regional health budgets.
  • 📌 Mandate hospital-local logistics partnerships for timely healthy meal delivery.
  • 📌 Outcome-based monitoring: Use app data to evaluate non-communicable disease prevention policies (diabetes, hypertension, post-op).
🌏 Pilot first in high-HDI provinces (e.g., Bali), then replicate with adjustments for tech & human resource readiness.
