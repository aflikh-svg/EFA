📑 Executive Summary – Final Report

Preparation of PDF Application Document for KPBU Waste Sector Project

TPA Bakung, Bandar Lampung City

Final version: 12 January 2021 | ARG Consultant Team

1. Background & Urgency

Bandar Lampung City faces limited government budget for waste management while waste volume escalates (~777–850 tons/day). TPA Bakung (operational since 1995) is overloaded, operating as open dumping, causing leachate pollution, odors, and fires. The Ministry of PUPR promotes PPP (Public-Private Partnership / KPBU) to revitalize TPA Bakung using Refuse Derived Fuel (RDF) or Waste-to-Energy (WtE) technologies. Urgency: extend landfill lifespan, reduce GHG emissions, improve public services, and attract private investment through Project Development Facility (PDF) from the Ministry of Finance.

2. Scope of Work

Preparation of the PDF application document for the KPBU project at TPA Bakung (14.1 Ha), Bakung Village, Teluk Betung Barat District, Bandar Lampung. Activities included: review of preliminary study, field surveys (institutional, real demand, observation), pre-feasibility studies (legal & institutional, technical, economic & commercial, environmental & social, PPP forms, risk, government support, follow-up actions), and two Focus Group Discussions (FGD).

3. Objectives

To produce a comprehensive and bankable PDF application document to submit to the Ministry of Finance, securing Project Development Facility (PDF) support for the TPA Bakung PPP waste project. The document serves as the basis for fiscal support, ensuring effective, efficient, and sustainable waste infrastructure.

4. Methodology

  • Approach: Integrated, systematic, applicative, rational.
  • Data collection: Questionnaires, in-depth interviews, field observation, secondary document review (incl. TPA Bakung Master Plan 2019).
  • Real Demand Survey (RDS): 220 household + 250 non-household respondents (willingness to pay).
  • Analysis: Qualitative & quantitative, gap analysis, financial analysis (NPV, IRR, DSCR, Payback), Social Cost-Benefit Analysis (ABMS), risk assessment (identification, allocation, mitigation).
  • Phases: Mobilization, research & design, preliminary study review, surveys, FGD-1, revision, FGD-2, final reporting.

5. Outputs

  • Complete PDF Application Document (aligned with MoF Reg.73/2018 & Bappenas Reg.2/2020) covering 8 studies: legal & institutional, technical, economic & commercial, environmental & social, PPP scheme, risk, government support/guarantee, follow-up.
  • Executive Summary report.
  • Selected technology: RDF + Landfilling (extends landfill life to 22.9 years vs 2.16 years without technology).
  • Business scheme & financing structure (CAPEX ~IDR 626.26 billion; OPEX ~IDR 39.9 billion/year).
  • Recommended PPP model: Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT) for 20 years.

6. Outcomes

  • RDF technology selected as primary option (more attractive to private sector, lower cost, economically feasible: IRR 13% > WACC 10.22%; positive NPV).
  • Proposed tipping fee IDR 89,394/ton (at 777.5 tons/day) with net revenue IDR 160,605/ton – affordable for Bandar Lampung’s budget.
  • Mapping of 7 potential off-takers (coal power plants, cement, paper, animal feed industries) for RDF product.
  • Indicative construction schedule: TPA revitalization (2022), RDF installation (2023/2024), commercial operation (2025).
  • Institutional framework for PPP: PJPK (Mayor of Bandar Lampung), KPBU Team, Private Partner (BUP), supported by MoF, Bappenas, PUPR, PT PII.

7. Impact

  • Environmental: Reduction of leachate, methane emissions, fires, soil/water pollution. Only 10% residue goes to landfill.
  • Socio-economic: Better public health, formal jobs for ~200 scavengers as sorters, reduced health and social aid costs.
  • Fiscal: Lower APBD burden for conventional waste management, revenue from RDF sales.
  • Strategic: Landfill life extension >20 years, supports national waste reduction & renewable energy targets.

8. Sustainability

Sustainability is ensured by: (1) 20-year KPBU contract; (2) VGF (Viability Gap Fund) and infrastructure guarantee from PT PII; (3) AMDAL and environmental permit; (4) New local regulation for tariff adjustment every 5 years; (5) Community and scavenger involvement; (6) Off-taker agreements; (7) Asset transfer to government post-concession. However, upstream waste governance (separation, collection) must be improved to guarantee consistent waste supply.

9. Lesson Learned & Policy Recommendations

  • Lesson 1: RDF is more feasible than WtE in early stages due to complex WtE regulations and high investment.
  • Lesson 2: Overloaded landfills require initial government support (state/local budget) before private entry — integrated VGF with construction timeline is critical.
  • Policy recommendations:
    • ✅ Accelerate new local regulation on waste retribution with inflation indexing.
    • ✅ Mandate AMDAL for all PPP waste projects using thermal/RDF technology.
    • ✅ Establish a special upstream-downstream risk management task force at local government level.
    • ✅ Standardize RDF product quality and facilitate long-term off-taker contracts (PLTU, cement).
    • ✅ Integrate scavengers into formal KPBU business model as sorters.
    • ✅ Develop a roadmap to increase waste collection coverage to >90% to secure supply.

10. Brief Policy Summary

Policy Brief – TPA Bakung PPP Project, Bandar Lampung
The Mayor of Bandar Lampung is recommended to: (a) issue revised decrees for PJPK and KPBU Team (Jan 2021); (b) conduct public consultation and DPRD exposure; (c) complete AMDAL and environmental permit (Mar–Jul 2021); (d) continue market sounding with 7 off-takers; (e) submit PDF and VGF application to the Ministry of Finance; (f) prepare a competitive tender for BOT scheme (20 years). This policy can become a replicable model for other cities facing urban waste emergencies through equitable and sustainable PPP mechanisms.
